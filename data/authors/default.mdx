I'm a data scientist who cares about the gap between a working model and a model that keeps working. I've fine-tuned LLMs for clinical reasoning at Optum, built recommendation systems at Amazon, and spent a fair bit of time writing the kind of evaluation harnesses that actually tell you when something breaks.

My background is in ECE (Manipal Institute of Technology), but I've spent most of my career writing Python and reading paper abstracts at midnight.

Things I've shipped:

- Built an automated benchmarking suite and uncertainty-aware error surfacing engine at Optum; ran 40+ large-scale experiments in under 5 days, cutting model iteration cycles to a third of what they were
- Engineered terabyte-scale feature pipelines over 6B+ data points at Amazon and shipped ranking model improvements (GNNs, TabTransformer, TabLLM) that moved Precision@1% and ROC AUC in a system where a tenth of a percent is a big deal
