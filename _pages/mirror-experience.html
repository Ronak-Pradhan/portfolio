--- permalink: /experience.md layout: none sitemap: false --- # Experience — {{ site.first_name }} {{ site.last_name }} Two production case studies from my time at Amazon and Optum. Each walks through the problem, approach, results with concrete numbers, stack, and what I would do differently. --- ## Domain-adapting LLMs for healthcare reasoning — Optum Data Scientist Intern · January 2024 – Present · Healthcare / NLP ### Context Optum's clinical reasoning workflows depend on accurately interpreting complex healthcare documents (discharge summaries, prior authorization narratives, clinical notes). Off-the-shelf LLMs underperform because they have not seen enough clinical language and they hallucinate on edge cases that matter clinically. ### Problem Build a domain-adapted LLM pipeline that can perform sophisticated reasoning on healthcare documents at production quality — specifically improve textual-entailment precision so downstream automation does not act on false positives. ### Approach 1. Literature scan — surveyed clinical-NLP and PEFT (LoRA, QLoRA, instruction tuning, BioClinical BERT family) to pick a starting point that balances quality and cost. 2. Data curation — built a high-quality entailment dataset from internal sources, with careful label-quality review. 3. Fine-tuning — specialized BioClinical BERT with `transformers` + `peft`, comparing full fine-tuning vs PEFT under the same compute budget. 4. Serving — wrapped the winning model in a Flask service, dockerized it, deployed it to AWS SageMaker as an asynchronous endpoint. 5. Evaluation — ran ablations on precision vs recall, error-sliced by document type, and surfaced slice-level metrics to stakeholders. ### Results - Significantly improved precision on textual-entailment without losing recall in the operating range. - Shipped a production async endpoint on SageMaker — not a notebook prototype. - Documented the eval methodology so future iterations can be benchmarked against the same harness. ### Stack PyTorch, HuggingFace `transformers` + `peft`, BioClinical BERT, Flask, Docker, AWS SageMaker (async endpoint), Python. --- ## Improving product retrieval for Amazon.in with GANs and LLMs — Amazon Applied Scientist Intern · February 2023 – July 2023 · Recommender systems / IR ### Context Product retrieval on Amazon.in is a classic retrieval-then-rank pipeline operating at terabyte scale. Even a fractional improvement in Precision@1%R moves real revenue at Amazon's volume, and cold-start — surfacing newly listed products with little behavioral signal — is one of the hardest parts of the system. ### Problem Push retrieval quality on three axes simultaneously: high-precision head, overall ROC AUC, and cold-start ROC AUC, without regressing latency or training cost. ### Approach 1. Research integration — rapidly absorbed recent retrieval / GAN / LLM literature; identified candidates likely to help with cold-start and query understanding. 2. Data engineering — built a PySpark pipeline over 100M+ rows / terabyte-scale behavioral logs, with careful negative sampling. 3. Modeling — fine-tuned advanced ML models including GANs and LLMs, tracking three eval slices in parallel. 4. Eval and analysis — produced ablation tables and error analyses; pulled actionable insights from model outputs. 5. Stakeholder review — circulated design and results to 10+ experienced scientists, took feedback, iterated, and shipped. ### Results - +0.4% Precision @ 1% Recall. - +0.2% ROC AUC. - +0.4% Cold-Start ROC AUC. - Detailed project documentation and a reproducible eval harness. ### Stack PyTorch, HuggingFace, GANs, LLMs, PySpark, SQL, Amazon-internal modeling and serving infrastructure. ### What I would do differently Invest earlier in eval-set quality before iterating on models. A chunk of the cycles in the middle of the project went to chasing apparent regressions that turned out to be eval noise. --- Last updated: {{ 'now' | date: '%B %d, %Y' }}.
